Other Okonomiyaki-like dishes

If you thought, this were all the okonomi dishes around - think again. Japan is full of different varieties of the famous dish. Some are made with pickled radish, some with beans or chicken. Just as you like it. Some very different versions are shown below.

Monjayaki

Monjayaki is kind of the Tokyo-style version of Okonomiyaki. The ingredients are similar, but more dashi and water is added, making it very mushy. You eat it directly from the hot plate with little spatulas.

Hirayachi

The Okinawa-originated dish is quite a simple distinguishable version of Okonomiyaki and usually cooked at home. It's made of flour, eggs, salt, pepper and green onions topped with Worchester sauce.

Fuchuyaki

Named after its place of origin, Fuchu, this Okonomiyaki-like dish is made with ground meat. It's more similar to the Hiroshima version.

Kakioko

Kakioko is a regional dish in Hinase, Okayama, and uses Oysters as its main ingredient. As oysters are the most important industry in this town, a lot of tourists flock in for this meal.

How to make Okonomiyaki yourself

You can easily make Okonomiyaki at home. The recipes are available above and you don't need a high-end japanese restaurant kitchen. If you have a teppan grill, that's good, but a simple pan will also help and get you some nice round okonomiyaki. The more exotic ingredients, like Dashi or Nagaimo, are usually available in Asia supermarkets.
